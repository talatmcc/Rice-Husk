From Waste to Wealth: A Sustainable Solution for Energy Using Rice Husk

China produces vast amounts of rice every year. In 2024, the total rice production in China was 207.5 million metric tons, of which 25% is left as rice huskâ€”a byproduct that is often wasted and contributes to environmental pollution. To tackle this problem, we propose a fully eco-friendly, zero-waste management process that uses rice husk to generate electricity, purify water, and produce activated carbon, a valuable material for the battery industry. Additionally, this process yields clean nitrogen gas and rice husk ash bricks, which help reduce room temperature and noise levels.

We also propose a modified version of a downdraft gasifier, specially designed to extract valuable carbon materials. By implementing this comprehensive process, we can generate energy, reduce carbon emissions in the environment, and gain economic benefits.
